# Bio

## Who Is Charles Coonce?

Charles Coonce is a Data Engineer I at Clearway Energy Group, one of the largest renewable energy operators in the U.S. He brings a unique background that combines hands-on trade experience, teaching, and a growing career in data engineering — a combination that gives him strong communication skills, practical problem-solving instincts, and a genuine passion for using data to drive decisions.

## Career Journey

Charles's path to data science was not a straight line, and that's one of his strengths. He started his career in the electrical trade, following a family legacy as a fourth-generation electrician. After earning a Bachelor of Arts in Telecommunications from Indiana University, he completed an electrical apprenticeship and worked as a journeyperson electrician in both Indianapolis and Helena, Montana.

During his time in the trade, Charles discovered a talent for teaching. He began helping fellow apprentices understand complex concepts, which led him to join the Montana Electrical Training Center as an instructor. He eventually advanced to the role of Assistant Training Director, where he managed curriculum, mentored instructors, and oversaw the growth of the program.

It was in that leadership role that Charles had a pivotal moment. Facing a decision about whether to hire an additional instructor, he gathered three years of enrollment data, class sizes, and pass rates, then built visualizations in Excel to present the case to the board. The data made the decision clear, and the board approved the hire on the spot. That experience sparked Charles's passion for data-driven decision-making and set him on a new career path.

## Education

Charles holds a **Bachelor of Arts in Telecommunications** from **Indiana University** (2011). Motivated to transition into data, he completed the **Google Data Analytics Professional Certificate** in January 2023, which reignited his academic curiosity and provided a foundation in analytical thinking.

He is currently pursuing a **Bachelor of Science in Data Science** with a focus on Business Analytics and a minor in Human Systems Engineering at **Arizona State University**, expected December 2025. Charles made the **Dean's List in Spring 2025**, reflecting his renewed academic focus and commitment to the field.

## Professional Experience

### Clearway Energy Group — Data Engineer I (May 2024 – Present)

Charles started at Clearway Energy Group as an intern and was promoted to Data Engineer I, working on the asset and energy management team. His responsibilities include designing and implementing data pipelines using Dagster, dbt, Polars, and Snowflake to automate the ingestion, transformation, and validation of renewable energy asset data across solar, wind, and battery storage portfolios. He builds VER (Variable Energy Resource) data pipelines integrating with energy solution APIs for operational metrics and performance tracking, and develops financial and market reporting workflows in Hex and Snowflake that blend Intercontinental Exchange energy data and internal forward curves for forecasting and asset management.

### OneAmerica Financial — Business Analyst Intern (May 2024 – August 2024)

At OneAmerica Financial, Charles led the migration of over 10 years of Google Analytics data from 67 websites by designing and deploying custom Python scripts. He also refined his requirements elicitation skills through structured stakeholder interviews and orchestrated the redesign of the IT intranet by synthesizing qualitative data from six cross-functional teams.

### Montana Electrical Training Center — Assistant Training Director (May 2022 – June 2023)

As Assistant Training Director, Charles managed curriculum development, mentored instructors, and led the data-driven hiring decision that catalyzed his career transition. He previously served as a Journeyperson Electrical Instructor from June 2018 to May 2022.

## What Sets Charles Apart

Charles's background gives him several qualities that are hard to teach: he knows how to explain complex ideas to non-technical audiences (from years of teaching), he understands what it means to build something real and solve problems under pressure (from the electrical trade), and he approaches data work with the rigor of someone who chose this path deliberately after a successful first career. He is not just technically capable — he communicates well, takes ownership of projects, and brings a mature, self-aware perspective to his work.
